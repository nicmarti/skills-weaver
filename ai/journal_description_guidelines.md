# Journal Description Guidelines

## Purpose

Journal descriptions provide detailed, visual context for AI image generation and improve journal readability. They transform simple event logs into vivid, cinematic scenes.

## Template Structure

Every description should follow this template:

**[Characters] + [Location] + [Action] + [Atmosphere] + [Visual Details]**

### Example Breakdown

**Content**: "Combat contre 3 gobelins" 

**Description (EN)**: "Aldric and Lyra battle three goblins in a torch-lit stone corridor, steel clashing against crude blades as shadows dance on moss-covered walls"

- **Characters**: Aldric and Lyra
- **Location**: stone corridor
- **Action**: battle, clashing
- **Atmosphere**: torch-lit, shadows dance
- **Visual Details**: crude blades, moss-covered walls

## Length Guidelines

- **Target**: 30-50 words (2-3 sentences)
- **Minimum**: 15 words (too short loses visual detail)
- **Maximum**: 80 words (too long dilutes focus)

**Why this range?** AI image generators work best with focused, specific prompts. 30-50 words provide enough detail without overwhelming the model.

## Best Practices

### 1. Be Specific

‚ùå **Vague**: "in a dark place"
‚úÖ **Specific**: "in a torch-lit stone corridor"

‚ùå **Generic**: "the fighter attacks"
‚úÖ **Specific**: "Aldric swings his longsword"

### 2. Use Character Names

Character names add personality and help the AI generate consistent imagery.

‚ùå **Impersonal**: "The party fights"
‚úÖ **Personal**: "Aldric, Lyra and Thorin fight"

‚ùå **Generic**: "A cleric heals"
‚úÖ **Specific**: "Adanel channels divine light to heal"

### 3. Show, Don't Tell

Use sensory details instead of abstract concepts.

‚ùå **Tell**: "it's very dark"
‚úÖ **Show**: "shadows dance on moss-covered walls"

‚ùå **Tell**: "the dragon is scary"
‚úÖ **Show**: "the red dragon's eyes glow like molten lava"

### 4. Present Tense, Third Person

This creates immediacy and cinematic feel.

‚ùå **Past tense**: "Aldric fought three goblins"
‚úÖ **Present tense**: "Aldric fights three goblins"

‚ùå **First person**: "We enter the dungeon"
‚úÖ **Third person**: "The party enters the dungeon"

### 5. Include Lighting

Lighting is crucial for AI image generation.

Examples: torch-lit, moonlit, flickering candlelight, ethereal glow, dawn light, shadowy, sunlit, lantern-lit

### 6. Use Visual, Cinematic Language

Think of describing a movie scene.

Good words: glinting, crumbling, weathered, gleaming, shadowy, ancient, massive, shimmering, flickering

### 7. Maintain Atmosphere

Each entry type has a mood - respect it.

- Combat: intense, dynamic
- Exploration: mysterious, atmospheric
- Loot: rewarding, glittering
- Discovery: surprising, revealing

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ‚ùå Too Short

"Combat in dungeon" (3 words - not enough detail)

### ‚ùå Too Long

"Aldric the human fighter level 2 with his +1 longsword and plate armor attacks three goblin warriors AC 15 with 7 HP each in a 10x10 stone corridor with torches every 5 feet..." (47 words but mostly mechanics)

### ‚ùå Pure Mechanics

"The party rolls initiative. Aldric hits AC 15 for 8 damage."

### ‚ùå Exact Numbers

"The party finds exactly 2,347 gold pieces, 12 gems, and 4 magic items"
Better: "The chest overflows with thousands of gold coins, glittering gems, and magical treasures"

### ‚ùå No Visual Context

"The party talks to the NPC about the quest"
Better: "The hooded stranger leans close across the tavern table, whispering urgent warnings as flickering firelight casts dancing shadows"

### ‚ùå Missing Atmosphere

"Aldric fights a goblin and wins"
Better: "Aldric's sword flashes in torchlight as the goblin falls, dark blood pooling on ancient stones"

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing a description, check:

- [ ] 30-50 words (15 minimum, 80 maximum)
- [ ] Character names used (when relevant)
- [ ] Location/setting described
- [ ] Lighting mentioned
- [ ] Action or mood appropriate to type
- [ ] Visual, cinematic language
- [ ] Present tense, third person
- [ ] No game mechanics or exact numbers
- [ ] Both EN and FR versions (if bilingual)
- [ ] Same structure and detail in both languages

## Usage in AI Enrichment

When using these guidelines for automatic enrichment:

1. **Read the content** (FR title)
2. **Identify the type** (combat, exploration, etc.)
3. **Check context** (party members, recent events, session info)
4. **Apply type-specific guidelines**
5. **Generate both EN and FR**
6. **Verify length** (30-50 words target)
7. **Check quality** against checklist

The goal is creating vivid, image-friendly descriptions that capture the essence of each moment in the adventure.
